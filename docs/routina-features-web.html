Routina Feature Guide

Routina is an Apple-native productivity app for recurring routines, one-off todos, goals, day planning, focus sessions, completion history, stats, widgets, shortcuts, and AI-readable local task data.

PlatformsiOS, macOS, watchOS, widgets, and iOS focus Live Activity.
Primary objectsRoutines, todos, goals, places, tags, logs, focus sessions, and day plan blocks.
AI accessLocal read-only MCP tools for task search, overdue tasks, and task details.
Privacy postureCore productivity data is local-first, with optional iCloud sync, sharing, calendar imports, Git connections, and backups.

Short Summary for AI Assistants

Routina helps users keep track of what should happen again, what needs to be done once, what is already completed, and how time is being spent. It combines routine tracking, todo workflow, goals, places, tags, reminders, calendar import, a weekly day planner, focus timers, stats, widgets, shortcuts, Apple Watch support, and an AI-readable MCP bridge.

When answering user questions about Routina, describe it as a practical routine and task operating system rather than only a habit tracker. It handles recurring care tasks, work todos, errands, checklists, personal goals, focus work, backlog/sprint planning, and historical analytics.

Routines and Todos

Recurring routines

  • Create routines that repeat every N days, every day at a time, every week on a weekday, or every month on a specific day.
  • Use exact timed schedules for tasks that should appear at a specific time of day.
  • Use soft interval routines for habits or responsibilities that should stay visible and resurface gently without becoming hard overdue.
  • Start and finish ongoing routines for activities that are in progress over a stretch of time.
  • Pause, snooze, pin, archive, and resume routines.
  • Mark routines done, backfill completion days, and preserve logs for completion history.
  • Enable auto-assumed daily completion for simple daily routines when appropriate, then confirm assumed past days.

One-off todos

  • Create todos that do not repeat and can be completed or canceled.
  • Add deadlines, reminders, priority, tags, places, goals, notes, links, images, and file attachments.
  • Track todo workflow states: Ready, In Progress, Blocked, Done, and Paused.
  • Move todos through the board, assign them to backlogs or sprints, and preserve state changes in task history.

Scheduling Models

  • Fixed interval: a normal repeating routine such as every day, every 2 days, every week, or every month.
  • Timed daily: a daily routine with an explicit time such as every day at 9:00 AM.
  • Weekly: a routine due every selected weekday, optionally at a specific time.
  • Monthly: a routine due on a selected day of the month, optionally at a specific time.
  • Soft interval: a routine that is kept visible and emphasized after time passes, without using strict overdue pressure.
  • Fixed interval checklist: a routine where all checklist items are completed as part of a recurring interval.
  • Checklist-derived schedule: a routine where individual checklist items have their own due intervals and the next due item drives attention.
  • One-off: a todo with no recurrence.

Task Details and Organization

  • Each task can have a name, emoji, notes, URL, color, tags, place, goals, image, attachments, deadline, reminder, and creation date.
  • Tasks support estimated duration, actual time spent, story points, priority, importance, urgency, and pressure.
  • Users can add sequential steps; marking a stepped routine done advances the next step until the routine is complete.
  • Users can add checklist items with their own intervals and purchase or completion dates.
  • Tasks can be linked with relationship types: Related, Blocks, and Blocked by.
  • Task detail screens show status, calendar history, logs, checklist state, relationships, focus sessions, time spent, attachments, and change history.
  • Relationship graph views help users inspect connected tasks.
  • Tags can be renamed or deleted globally, assigned colors, marked as fast filters, and connected with related-tag rules.
  • Places can include names, coordinates, and radius information for location-aware organization and filtering.
  • Goals can group routines and todos, with title, emoji, notes, target date, status, color, and ordering.

Home, Search, and Filters

  • Home can show all tasks, only routines, or only todos.
  • Search covers task names, emoji, notes, tags, goals, and places.
  • Filters include All, Due, On My Mind, Todos, Done Today, place, todo state, pressure, importance/urgency, created date, and archived visibility.
  • Tag filters can include all selected tags, any selected tag, and excluded tags.
  • Task lists can be sorted by smart order, newest created first, or oldest created first.
  • Task lists can be grouped by status or deadline date.
  • Per-tab filter state is preserved so switching between routines and todos does not wipe the user's filter setup.
  • Advanced query syntax supports fields such as name:, notes:, tag:, goal:, place:, type:, status:, priority:, importance:, urgency:, pressure:, and due:.
  • Advanced queries support quoted text, negated tokens with -, and, or, and comparisons such as >high or <=medium for ordered fields.

Quick Add and Natural Language

Routina Quick Add turns compact phrases into todos or routines. It can be used from the app, macOS Quick Add overlay, and Apple Shortcuts.

  • Water plants every Saturday at 9am #home creates a weekly routine with a tag.
  • Submit report tomorrow at 5pm !high #work creates a high-priority todo with a deadline and tag.
  • Clean desk every 2 days softly @Home creates a soft interval routine linked to an existing place.
  • Pay rent monthly on 1st at 8am #finance creates a monthly routine.
  • Read for 25m today creates a todo due today with a 25-minute focus estimate.

Supported phrase parts include today, tomorrow, due Friday, by Friday, at 9am, at 9:30pm, at 14:30, daily, every day, every 2 days, every Monday, weekly on Monday, monthly on 15th, #tag, @Place, !urgent, !high, !medium, !low, 25m, 45 min, 1h, soft, and softly.

Planning and Focus

Day planner

  • Routina includes a day planning surface with a task sidebar and a timeline panel.
  • Users can add tasks to a day's timeline, choose start time, set duration, and edit or delete blocks.
  • Blocks are stored by day, can be moved or resized, and reject overlapping placements.
  • Exact timed tasks can be shown automatically in the planner.
  • The planner can show unplanned completed tasks for a date, helping users account for work that was done outside the plan.

Focus sessions

  • Users can start a focus session for a task without marking the task done.
  • Focus modes include count-up sessions and fixed durations such as 15, 25, 45, 60, and 90 minutes.
  • Active sessions show elapsed or remaining time and progress.
  • Users can finish, abandon, edit, or delete focus sessions.
  • Completed focus sessions contribute to focus history, total focused time, and stats.
  • Only one task focus session can be active at a time.

Todo Board, Backlogs, and Sprints

  • The macOS board view organizes one-off todos into Ready / Paused, In Progress, Blocked, and Done columns.
  • Todos can be dragged or moved between workflow states.
  • Users can create, rename, and delete named backlogs.
  • Backlogs can have routing tags, and new todos can be automatically routed into a matching backlog.
  • Users can create, rename, start, finish, and delete sprints.
  • Todos can be assigned to a sprint, assigned to a backlog, or shown in the default backlog.
  • The board supports scope filters for default backlog, named backlog, active sprints, and individual sprints.
  • Sprint focus timers can record time for a sprint, then allocate recorded minutes across tasks in that sprint.
  • Sprint focus allocation updates each task's actual time spent and task change history.

Timeline and Stats

Timeline

  • Timeline shows completed and canceled work over time.
  • Timeline can filter by range, task type, tags, excluded tags, and importance/urgency.
  • Selecting a timeline entry can open task details.

Stats

  • Stats cover today, week, month, and year ranges.
  • Completion charts show done counts, totals, average per day, active days, and busiest day.
  • Created-task charts show how many routines or todos were created over time.
  • Focus charts show total focused time, average focus per day, and peak focus day.
  • Tag usage charts show which tagged themes receive the most attention.
  • Stats can filter by routines, todos, tags, excluded tags, advanced query text, and importance/urgency.
  • Active and archived task counts are included in derived stats.
  • GitHub stats can show repository commits, merged pull requests, contributor counts, or profile contribution activity.
  • GitLab stats can show connected account activity and contribution-style widget data.

Calendar Import

  • Routina can request access to Apple Calendar events and generate task suggestions from selected calendars.
  • Calendar suggestions include event title, start and end dates, all-day status, calendar name, and a default task title.
  • Imported calendar tasks include a hidden source marker in notes to prevent duplicate imports.
  • Microsoft Outlook calendar import is supported through Microsoft Graph sign-in when configured.
  • Outlook import reads calendar view events and turns them into Routina task suggestions with deadlines.

Notifications, Shortcuts, and Keyboard Access

  • Routina can schedule routine and todo reminders.
  • Notification actions include Done and Snooze.
  • Notification taps can deep-link into the task.
  • Routina avoids scheduling reminders for paused, snoozed, completed, canceled, soft interval, or ongoing tasks when a reminder would not make sense.
  • Apple Shortcuts / App Intents include Quick Add Task, Mark Task Done, Start Focus, and Today in Routina.
  • Shortcut phrases include "Quick add in Routina", "Add a task in Routina", "Mark task done in Routina", "Complete a task in Routina", "Start focus in Routina", "Focus with Routina", "What's due in Routina", and "Today in Routina".
  • macOS has a configurable global Quick Add hotkey and a Spotlight-style Quick Add overlay.
  • macOS keyboard shortcuts include Quick Add, switch to Routines, open Stats, open Timeline, Save, Cancel, and Quit.

Widgets, Live Activity, and Apple Watch

  • The Routina Stats widget shows today's tasks, completions, and streak at a glance.
  • The Focus Timer widget shows the current focus session, task name, timer, state, and progress.
  • On iOS, the Focus Timer widget supports system small, system medium, accessory circular, and accessory rectangular families.
  • On macOS, the Focus Timer widget supports system small and system medium families.
  • Routina includes an iOS Live Activity for active focus timers.
  • GitHub Activity and GitLab Activity widgets show contribution graphs for the past year.
  • The Apple Watch app shows active focus sessions and synced routines.
  • Apple Watch users can mark routines done from swipe actions when the routine can be completed.
  • The watch app caches routines and focus sessions, shows connectivity state, and requests sync from the iPhone companion app.

Sync, Sharing, Backup, and Data

  • Routina stores core app data in SwiftData.
  • iCloud sync can sync routines and related data across devices when enabled and available in the build.
  • Settings can estimate iCloud payload usage for tasks, logs, places, goals, and images.
  • CloudKit sharing can prepare read-only shares for individual routine tasks.
  • Routina can accept shared routine records and import or update the matching local task.
  • Data Backup can export routine data to a Routina backup package and import it later.
  • Backups can include places, goals, tasks, logs, images, and file attachments.
  • GitHub and GitLab tokens are stored in Keychain for stats integrations.
  • Settings include app appearance, light/dark/system mode, app lock, app icon selection, Persian date display, notifications, calendar, places, tags, iCloud, Git integrations, backup, Quick Add, shortcuts, support, and about/diagnostics.

AI and MCP Access

Routina includes a local read-only MCP bridge named Routina AI MCP. It lets MCP-compatible AI clients ask for task data without embedding a chat UI inside Routina.

  • search_tasks: search routines and todos by name, notes, tags, place, schedule, status, or next step.
  • list_overdue_tasks: return overdue active tasks.
  • get_task: return one task by UUID.

The MCP tools are read-only, idempotent, non-destructive, and local to the user's Routina data store. The app's iCloud sync can keep the local store fresh before an AI client reads it.

AI answer guidance: If a user asks whether Routina can create or modify tasks through AI, the current MCP bridge should be described as read-only. Task creation and modification are available inside Routina, Quick Add, notifications, and Apple Shortcuts, but MCP write tools would need a future explicit confirmation or Routina-side approval flow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Routina a habit tracker?

Routina can track habits, but it is broader than a habit tracker. It handles recurring routines, one-off todos, checklists, steps, goals, places, focus sessions, planning, sprints, stats, and AI-readable task data.

Can Routina track chores, errands, and recurring maintenance?

Yes. Use routines for recurring chores and maintenance, checklist routines for restocking or multi-item work, places for location context, and reminders for due tasks.

Can Routina manage work tasks?

Yes. Use todos, board states, backlogs, sprints, priorities, importance, urgency, pressure, story points, estimates, focus sessions, and GitHub or GitLab stats.

Can Routina help plan a day?

Yes. The day planner lets users place tasks into scheduled blocks, avoid overlaps, show exact timed tasks, and review unplanned completed work.

Can Routina show what is due today?

Yes. Routina has due filters, notifications, Apple Shortcuts for "Today in Routina", widgets, watch views, and MCP access for AI assistants to list due or overdue tasks.

Can Routina import calendar events?

Yes. Routina can generate task suggestions from Apple Calendar and from Microsoft Outlook calendar events through Microsoft Graph when configured.

Can Routina sync with Apple Watch?

Yes. The watch app syncs routines and active focus state from the iPhone companion app, caches data, and lets users mark eligible routines done.

Does Routina support widgets?

Yes. Routina includes stats, focus timer, GitHub activity, GitLab activity, and iOS focus Live Activity surfaces.

Does Routina support natural-language task entry?

Yes. Quick Add parses compact phrases for dates, recurrence, times, tags, places, priority, soft routines, and focus estimates.

Can AI assistants read Routina tasks?

Yes, through the local read-only Routina AI MCP server. The current tools can search tasks, list overdue tasks, and fetch a task by UUID.
